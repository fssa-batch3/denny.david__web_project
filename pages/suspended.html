Suspended chords

Suspended chords are formed by replacing the third of a chord with either the second (sus2) or the fourth (sus4) note of the scale. For example, a C major chord with the third replaced by the second would be written as Csus2 (C, D, G), while a C major chord with the third replaced by the fourth would be written as Csus4 (C, F, G).

Suspended chords are often used as a way to add tension and release to a chord progression. Because they don't contain the "stable" third note of a chord, they create a sense of uncertainty that can be resolved by returning to a more "conventional" chord.

Suspended chords can also be used to create interesting harmonies in their own right. Depending on the context and the melody, a sus chord can sound melancholic, uplifting, or simply unusual.
